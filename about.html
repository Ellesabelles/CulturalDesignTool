Designing for Diverse Cultures

About the Tool

This tool is part of a class project for the International User Experience And Communication class taught by Professor Noske at University of Washington's Human-Centered Design & Engineering Master's program. In this class, we study how to use research methods to develop insight into other cultures, and how design choices can be informed by an understanding of diverse cultures. One of the tools that we used in this class was the "Cultural Dimensions Theory" of Geert Hofstede and subsequent authors who built upon his theories. Cultural dimensions theory seeks to isolate specific paradigms in culture and compare them across national boundaries so that certain aspects of behavior and cognition can be more deeply understood.

Authorship

The intellectual content in this tool is sourced from the work of social scientist Geert Hofstede,designer Aaron Marcus and his associate, Emilie Gould. This tool references various writings (see the references section) and although care has been taken in representing their work faithfully, it is possible that some aspects of their work have been misrepresented through a lack of understanding. Please consult their original works for any questions on their views.
